# Generated: 2026-03-28
# Feasibility: Quick-check PASSED (14+ RCTs, clear gap, no competing NMA found)

## Research Question
What is the comparative efficacy and safety of all currently available and
investigational HIV pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) interventions — including
oral agents, long-acting injectables, and the dapivirine vaginal ring — versus
placebo or active comparator among HIV-uninfected adults at risk of HIV
acquisition, as assessed by network meta-analysis?

## PICO Elements

**Population**: HIV-uninfected adults (aged >=18) at risk of HIV-1 acquisition,
including cisgender men who have sex with men (MSM), transgender women (TGW),
transgender men, non-binary individuals, cisgender women, and heterosexual
serodiscordant couples; any geographic setting.

**Interventions** (all active arms to be included as NMA nodes):
1. TDF/FTC - tenofovir disoproxil fumarate/emtricitabine (daily oral) [approved]
2. TAF/FTC - tenofovir alafenamide/emtricitabine (daily oral) [approved]
3. TDF monotherapy (daily oral) [approved, comparator arm in Partners PrEP]
4. TDF/FTC on-demand (2-1-1 event-driven oral) [approved in some jurisdictions]
5. CAB-LA - long-acting injectable cabotegravir (bimonthly/every 2 months) [approved]
6. Lenacapavir injectable (twice-yearly subcutaneous) [FDA-approved June 2025]
7. Dapivirine vaginal ring (monthly intravaginal ring) [EMA-approved; not FDA-approved]
8. Placebo / no active PrEP (standard of care + risk counseling)

**Comparator**: Placebo (blinded), active comparator (open-label or blinded), or
standard HIV prevention services only; network will link arms via common comparators.

**Outcomes**:
- Primary: HIV-1 incidence (confirmed new infections per 100 person-years at risk)
- Secondary:
  * Grade 3/4 adverse events (any)
  * Serious adverse events (SAEs)
  * Discontinuation due to adverse events
  * Adherence/drug exposure (drug levels where measured)
  * Drug resistance among incident infections

## Study Design
Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) only.
- Phase 2b, Phase 3, and Phase 3b/4 trials eligible
- Open-label and blinded designs eligible
- Minimum follow-up: 24 weeks
- No language restriction

## Network Structure (Expected Edges)
- Placebo ↔ TDF/FTC: iPrEx, TDF2, FEM-PrEP, VOICE, Partners PrEP
- Placebo ↔ TDF: Partners PrEP, VOICE
- Placebo ↔ Dapivirine ring: ASPIRE (MTN-020), Ring Study (IPM 027)
- TDF/FTC ↔ TAF/FTC: DISCOVER
- TDF/FTC ↔ CAB-LA: HPTN 083, HPTN 084
- TDF/FTC ↔ Lenacapavir: PURPOSE 2; TAF/FTC ↔ Lenacapavir: PURPOSE 1
- TDF/FTC (on-demand) ↔ Placebo: IPERGAY
- Open-label TDF/FTC ↔ Deferred: PROUD

## Feasibility Notes (from brainstorming 2026-03-28)
- Estimated RCTs: ~14 eligible trials (likely 12-16 after full screening)
- Existing NMAs: None found comparing ALL modalities for efficacy
  (existing NMAs focus on adherence interventions only)
- Heterogeneity risk: MODERATE
  * Populations: MSM, women, heterosexual couples (plan subgroup analysis)
  * Comparators: Placebo and active comparator (address via NMA design)
  * Adherence confounding: FEM-PrEP/VOICE showed no efficacy due to non-adherence
    (include sensitivity analysis excluding adherence-confounded trials)
- Outcome reporting: EXCELLENT (all trials report HIV incidence as primary)
- Data extractability: EXCELLENT (binary outcome, events/person-time available)

## Analysis Type
**NMA (Network Meta-Analysis)**
Justification: >=3 distinct intervention nodes with connected comparisons.
Approach: Bayesian random-effects NMA (gemtc/BUGSnet or frequentist netmeta)
Geometry: Star + loop network via TDF/FTC as central node

## Planned Subgroup / Sensitivity Analyses
1. By population (MSM/TGW vs. women vs. heterosexual)
2. By adherence (exclude trials with confirmed non-adherence: FEM-PrEP, VOICE)
3. By risk of bias (low vs. high)
4. By blinding (double-blind vs. open-label)
5. Node-splitting for inconsistency assessment

## Potential Challenges
1. Populations differ substantially across trials (MSM vs women)
   → Mitigation: Subgroup NMA by sex/risk group; transitivity assumption testing
2. Adherence confounding (FEM-PrEP, VOICE) may distort placebo-arm rates
   → Mitigation: Primary analysis per-protocol or sensitivity excluding these
3. PROUD uses deferred (not placebo) as control - indirect comparison only
   → Mitigation: Include as observational note; exclude from primary NMA
4. On-demand vs daily TDF/FTC differ pharmacologically
   → Mitigation: Treat as separate node
5. Lenacapavir PURPOSE 1 had NO events in active arm — RR = 0
   → Mitigation: Use incidence rate ratio (Poisson) or use continuity correction
