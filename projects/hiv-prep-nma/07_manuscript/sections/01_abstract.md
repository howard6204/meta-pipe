# Abstract

## Background
Multiple HIV pre-exposure prophylaxis (PrEP) modalities — including daily oral
agents, long-acting injectables, and the dapivirine vaginal ring — have
demonstrated efficacy in randomized controlled trials (RCTs). However, no
comprehensive network meta-analysis (NMA) has directly compared all available
and investigational PrEP interventions, including not-yet-universally-approved
agents, in a unified evidence synthesis.

## Methods
We conducted a systematic review and NMA following PRISMA-NMA guidelines.
We searched PubMed/MEDLINE, Embase, Cochrane CENTRAL, ClinicalTrials.gov, and
conference abstracts from 2000 to March 2026 for RCTs evaluating any PrEP
intervention among HIV-uninfected adults at risk of HIV-1 acquisition.
The primary outcome was confirmed HIV-1 incidence per 100 person-years.
We performed frequentist random-effects NMA using the graph-theoretic approach
with reference to placebo, generated Surface Under Cumulative Ranking (SUCRA)
values, and assessed inconsistency using node-splitting. Predefined subgroup
analyses by risk population (MSM/transgender vs cisgender women vs heterosexual)
and sensitivity analyses excluding adherence-confounded trials were conducted.

## Results
Fourteen RCTs encompassing 31 trial arms, 50,743 participants, and 8
intervention nodes were included. Between-study heterogeneity was moderate
(tau2 = 0.051, tau = 0.23; I2 = 52.3%). Most active interventions significantly
reduced HIV incidence versus placebo. Lenacapavir ranked highest (SUCRA 99.1%;
IRR 0.03, 95% CI 0.008–0.099; efficacy 97%), followed by CAB-LA (SUCRA 79.2%;
IRR 0.14, 95% CI 0.069–0.273; efficacy 86%), TDF/FTC on-demand (SUCRA 76.2%;
IRR 0.14, 95% CI 0.031–0.680; efficacy 86%), TAF/FTC daily (SUCRA 47.6%;
IRR 0.50, 95% CI 0.277–0.899; efficacy 50%), TDF/FTC daily (SUCRA 39.5%;
IRR 0.58, 95% CI 0.440–0.755; efficacy 42%), TDF monotherapy (SUCRA 34.2%;
IRR 0.61, 95% CI 0.430–0.864; efficacy 39%), and dapivirine vaginal ring
(SUCRA 23.5%; IRR 0.71, 95% CI 0.484–1.028; p = 0.069). Rankings were
consistent in sensitivity analyses excluding adherence-confounded trials
(FEM-PrEP, VOICE) and in subgroup analyses by population. No significant
inconsistency was detected by node-splitting.

## Conclusions
Twice-yearly injectable lenacapavir ranks highest for HIV prevention efficacy,
followed by CAB-LA. Long-acting injectables address the critical barrier of
daily pill adherence. The dapivirine vaginal ring offers a woman-controlled
prevention option with a modest, non-statistically-significant reduction in the
NMA estimate, likely attributable to dilution by adherence heterogeneity.
These findings support individualized, patient-centred PrEP choices guided by
efficacy, route of administration, regulatory availability, and access.
