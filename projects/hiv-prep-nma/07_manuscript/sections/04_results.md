# Results

## Study Selection
The systematic search identified 847 records, of which 723 remained after
deduplication. Following title/abstract screening, 48 full-text articles were
reviewed, and 14 RCTs met eligibility criteria (Figure 1: PRISMA flow diagram).
Two records were excluded for non-randomized design (PREVENIR cohort) and two
for wrong study type. The pre-identified seed set of 14 anchor trials was
confirmed to capture all eligible evidence.

## Study Characteristics (Table 1)
The 14 included trials enrolled 31 trial arms and a total of approximately 50,743
participants across Africa, the Americas, Europe, and Asia-Pacific. Follow-up
duration ranged from 52 to 144 weeks. Seven trials enrolled MSM/transgender
women predominantly (iPrEx, IPERGAY, PROUD, DISCOVER, HPTN 083, PURPOSE 2),
six enrolled cisgender women (FEM-PrEP, VOICE, ASPIRE, Ring Study, HPTN 084,
PURPOSE 1), and one enrolled heterosexual serodiscordant couples (Partners PrEP).
Background HIV incidence in placebo/control arms ranged from 0.46 (IPERGAY) to
6.31 per 100 person-years (PROUD deferred arm; Ring Study placebo), reflecting
variation in risk profiles.

**Intervention nodes and contributing trials:**
- **Placebo**: iPrEx, Partners PrEP, TDF2, FEM-PrEP, VOICE, IPERGAY, ASPIRE, Ring Study (8 trials)
- **TDF/FTC daily oral**: iPrEx, Partners PrEP, TDF2, FEM-PrEP, VOICE, PROUD, DISCOVER (comparator), HPTN 083 (comparator), HPTN 084 (comparator), PURPOSE 1 (comparator), PURPOSE 2 (comparator)
- **TDF monotherapy daily**: Partners PrEP, VOICE
- **TDF/FTC on-demand**: IPERGAY
- **TAF/FTC daily oral**: DISCOVER, PURPOSE 1
- **CAB-LA injectable**: HPTN 083, HPTN 084
- **Lenacapavir (twice-yearly)**: PURPOSE 1, PURPOSE 2
- **Dapivirine vaginal ring**: ASPIRE, Ring Study

## Risk of Bias
All 14 trials were assessed as low overall risk of bias using RoB 2. PROUD was
rated high risk in the blinding domain (open-label design). FEM-PrEP and VOICE
were rated high risk in the "deviations from intended interventions" domain due to
confirmed non-adherence confirmed as the primary cause of futility (plasma
drug levels detected in <40% and <30% of participants, respectively).

## Network Structure
The network geometry (Figure 1) shows a well-connected hub-and-spoke structure
with TDF/FTC as the central node, linking both placebo-controlled and
active-controlled trials. Direct comparisons exist for all 8 nodes. The
dapivirine ring has direct connections to placebo (ASPIRE, Ring Study) and is
linked to all other nodes through TDF/FTC, enabling indirect comparisons with
lenacapavir and CAB-LA.

## Primary Outcome: HIV-1 Incidence (NMA Results)

Random-effects NMA included 31 arms from 14 RCTs. Between-study heterogeneity
was moderate (tau2 = 0.051, tau = 0.23; I2 = 52.3%; Q = 25.16, df = 12,
p = 0.014). All interventions except the dapivirine vaginal ring reached
statistical significance versus placebo. Results are summarised in Table 2 and
Figure 2 (NMA forest plot).

The complete league table of all pairwise NMA estimates is presented in
Supplementary Table S1 (Figure 4). Notably, lenacapavir was superior to
TDF/FTC daily (IRR 0.05, 95% CI 0.01–0.18) and to the dapivirine ring
(IRR 0.04, 95% CI 0.01–0.21). The comparison of lenacapavir versus CAB-LA
favoured lenacapavir (IRR 0.20) but the 95% CI crossed unity, reflecting
uncertainty in this indirect estimate.

## Heterogeneity
Between-study heterogeneity was moderate (tau2 = 0.051, I2 = 52.3%).
The highest contribution to heterogeneity arose from the TDF/FTC vs placebo
node, particularly the adherence-confounded trials (FEM-PrEP, VOICE, iPrEx
low-adherence subset), reflecting behavioural rather than pharmacological
differences.

## Inconsistency
Node-splitting found no statistically significant inconsistency in any closed
loop (all p_inconsistency > 0.10). The funnel plot (Figure 6) showed no
clear asymmetry, with most comparisons centred near the pooled estimate,
indicating no evidence of substantial small-study effects or publication bias.

## Sensitivity Analyses
Excluding adherence-confounded trials (FEM-PrEP, VOICE) reduced heterogeneity
and strengthened the TDF/FTC estimate, consistent with the expected impact of
per-protocol adherence. Rankings of lenacapavir and CAB-LA were stable across
all sensitivity analyses.

## Subgroup Analyses

### MSM/Transgender Women
In the MSM/TGW subgroup (iPrEx, IPERGAY, PROUD, DISCOVER, HPTN 083, PURPOSE 2),
lenacapavir and CAB-LA ranked first and second respectively. TDF/FTC on-demand
showed high efficacy comparable to daily oral agents in this population, where
coitally predictable exposure supports the 2-1-1 strategy.

### Cisgender Women
In the women-only subgroup (FEM-PrEP, VOICE, ASPIRE, Ring Study, HPTN 084,
PURPOSE 1), lenacapavir ranked first (SUCRA 99.1%) followed by CAB-LA
(SUCRA 96.2%). The dapivirine vaginal ring showed consistent though more modest
efficacy in this subgroup, consistent with ASPIRE and Ring Study results.
TDF/FTC on-demand was not evaluable in women (no supporting trial data).

## Safety
Grade 3/4 adverse events and serious adverse events were comparable across all
active PrEP interventions. Injection-site reactions were the most common adverse
effect with CAB-LA but rarely led to discontinuation (<1%). No new safety
signals emerged with lenacapavir in PURPOSE 1 or PURPOSE 2. Drug-resistant HIV
at failure was rare and isolated; one of two seroconverters in PURPOSE 2
(lenacapavir arm) had pre-existing integrase strand transfer inhibitor resistance.
