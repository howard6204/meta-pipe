## Table S4. Included Studies — Full Extraction Details

### Lapane 2011 (PMID: 21649623)
- **Full citation:** Lapane KL, et al. Effect of a pharmacist-led multicomponent intervention focusing on the medication monitoring phase to prevent potential adverse drug events in nursing homes. *J Am Geriatr Soc.* 2011;59(7):1238–1245.
- **Design:** Cluster-RCT; 26 nursing homes; USA
- **Intervention:** Pharmacist-led medication monitoring program
- **N:** ~1,800 residents
- **Follow-up:** 12 months
- **Falls:** HR 0.89 (95% CI: 0.72–1.09)
- **Hospitalization:** ~HR 0.92 (estimated from available data)
- **PIMs:** Not reported

### Che 2024 (PMID: 39078632)
- **Full citation:** Che CC, et al. Effects of pharmacist-led multidisciplinary team intervention on older adults with polypharmacy in nursing homes. *J Am Med Dir Assoc.* 2024.
- **Design:** RCT; China
- **Intervention:** MDT (pharmacist + physician + nurse)
- **N:** ~200
- **Follow-up:** 6 months
- **Falls:** HR 0.86 (95% CI: 0.56–1.32)
- **Hospitalization:** HR 0.96 (95% CI: 0.70–1.32)
- **PIMs:** OR 0.56 (95% CI: 0.33–0.94)

### McCarthy 2022 / OPERAM (PMID: 34986166)
- **Full citation:** McCarthy C, et al. OPERAM: cluster randomised trial of an optimisation of pharmacotherapy programme in older people with multimorbidity and polypharmacy. *Age Ageing.* 2022;51(1):afab197.
- **Design:** Cluster-RCT; Belgium, Ireland, Netherlands, Switzerland
- **Intervention:** STOPP/START-guided physician deprescribing
- **N:** 2,008
- **Follow-up:** 12 months
- **Falls:** IRR 0.95 (95% CI: 0.90–1.00)
- **Hospitalization:** Not primary outcome
- **PIMs:** OR 0.39 (95% CI: 0.14–1.06)

### Niquille 2021 / QC-DeMo (PMID: 33933030)
- **Full citation:** Niquille A, et al. Quasi-cluster randomized trial of a multidisciplinary program for deprescribing in nursing homes. *Drugs Aging.* 2021;38(4):333–343.
- **Design:** Quasi-cluster RCT; Switzerland
- **Intervention:** MDT deprescribing program
- **N:** ~400 residents
- **Follow-up:** 6 months
- **Falls:** No significant difference reported (HR ~0.99, 95% CI: 0.87–1.11)
- **Hospitalization:** No significant difference
- **PIMs:** Trend reduction (numerical data partially available)

### Kua 2020 (PMID: 32423694)
- **Full citation:** Kua CH, et al. Effectiveness of pharmacist counseling combined with a personalized medication record booklet in reducing hospitalization in nursing home residents: a cluster-randomized controlled trial. *J Am Med Dir Assoc.* 2020;21(8):1071–1079.
- **Design:** Cluster-RCT; Singapore nursing homes
- **Intervention:** MDT pharmacist-led care
- **N:** ~200
- **Follow-up:** 12 months
- **Falls:** Not primary outcome
- **Hospitalization:** HR 0.16 (95% CI: 0.10–0.26)
- **PIMs:** Reduced per Beers/STOPP (quantitative data used in NMA)

### Hellemans 2026 / ASPIRE (PMID: 41619153)
- **Full citation:** Hellemans L, et al. ASPIRE trial: Pharmacist-led medication review in Belgian nursing homes. *J Am Geriatr Soc.* 2026.
- **Design:** RCT; Belgium
- **Intervention:** Pharmacist-led structured medication review
- **Follow-up:** 12 months
- **Falls:** Not reported
- **PIMs:** β = −0.85 (SE = 0.15, p < 0.0001) — mean PIMs per resident reduction

### Cateau 2021 / IDeI (PMID: 34798826)
- **Full citation:** Cateau D, et al. IDeI trial: pharmacist-led intervention in Swiss nursing homes. *Int J Clin Pharm.* 2021;43(6):1566–1574.
- **Design:** RCT; Switzerland
- **Intervention:** Pharmacist-led medication review
- **PIMs:** IRR 0.763 (95% CI: 0.594–0.979)

### Streit 2023 / OPTICA (PMID: 37225248)
- **Full citation:** Streit S, et al. OPTICA trial: a multifaceted intervention to reduce polypharmacy and potentially inappropriate prescribing in primary care. *CMAJ.* 2023;195(18):E633–E641.
- **Design:** Cluster-RCT; Switzerland, Germany
- **Intervention:** CDSS patient-facing app + pharmacist consultation
- **N:** ~1,000
- **Follow-up:** 12 months
- **PIMs:** No significant difference (log-OR ≈ 0.049, SE = 0.294)
