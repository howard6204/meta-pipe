# Meta-Analysis Topic
# Generated: 2026-03-27
# Type: Network Meta-Analysis (NMA)

## Research Question
Which polypharmacy management intervention most effectively reduces falls and
unplanned hospitalizations in older adults? A network meta-analysis comparing
pharmacist-led reviews, clinical decision support systems (CDSS), multidisciplinary
team (MDT) programs, physician-led deprescribing, and usual care.

## PICO Elements

**Population**: Older adults (≥65 years) receiving ≥5 medications (polypharmacy)
  - Subgroup: Institutionalized (nursing home / long-term care) vs. Community-dwelling
**Intervention (Nodes)**:
  1. Pharmacist-led medication review (Pharmacist)
  2. Computerized / electronic clinical decision support system (CDSS)
  3. Multidisciplinary team (MDT) — includes pharmacist + physician + nurse
  4. Physician-led deprescribing (Deprescribing)
  5. Usual care (UC) — network reference node
**Comparator**: Usual care (UC) and all pairwise comparisons
**Outcomes**:
  - PRIMARY  (NMA-1): Falls rate / incidence — RR or IRR
  - PRIMARY  (NMA-1): Hospitalization rate — RR or IRR
  - SECONDARY (NMA-2): PIMs reduction — RR or MD (count or proportion)
    - Subgroup: European studies vs. Asian studies

## Study Design
- Randomized controlled trials (RCTs) and cluster-RCTs
- Minimum 3 months follow-up
- Exclude: single-drug interventions, non-elderly populations, no control arm

## Expected Network Geometry
- Moderately connected (not star-shaped)
- Most trials compare single intervention vs. UC
- Some trials may compare MDT vs. Pharmacist or CDSS
- Estimated studies: 20–40 RCTs

## Analysis Plan

### NMA-1: Falls & Hospitalization
- Effect measure: RR (binary outcomes) or IRR (rate outcomes)
- Model: Bayesian (gemtc primary) + Frequentist (netmeta sensitivity)
- Heterogeneity threshold: I² > 50% → automatic subgroup analysis
  - Subgroup A: Institutionalized elderly (nursing home, LTCF)
  - Subgroup B: Community-dwelling elderly
- Output: Network plot, SUCRA table, rankogram, league table, forest plots

### NMA-2: PIMs Reduction
- Effect measure: RR (proportion of patients with ≥1 PIM reduced)
- Stratified analysis: European studies vs. Asian studies
- Same Bayesian framework as NMA-1
- PIM criteria: Beers (North America), STOPP/START (Europe), Screening Tool of
  Older Persons' potentially inappropriate Prescriptions (STOPP) for Asia where
  applicable

## Heterogeneity Management
- I² < 25%: Fixed-effects or consistency model
- I² 25–50%: Random-effects, flag moderate heterogeneity
- I² > 50%: Trigger subgroup analysis (setting for NMA-1; region for NMA-2)
- Node-splitting for inconsistency assessment
