# Eligibility Criteria — Polypharmacy NMA

## Inclusion Criteria

### Population
- Adults aged ≥65 years (or mean age ≥65 if age range not specified)
- Polypharmacy defined as ≥5 chronic medications (or study-defined polypharmacy explicitly stated)
- Setting: any (community, primary care, nursing home, hospital-based outpatient)

### Intervention
At least one of the five network nodes:
1. **Pharmacist-led**: Pharmacist conducting structured medication review ± recommendations
2. **CDSS**: Electronic/computerised clinical decision support generating medication alerts
3. **MDT**: Team-based intervention involving ≥2 disciplines (must include physician + ≥1 other HCP)
4. **Deprescribing**: Physician-initiated systematic process to reduce/discontinue medications
5. **Usual care**: No structured medication review beyond routine clinical care

### Comparator
- Active or passive comparator from within the five nodes
- Cross-comparisons permitted (e.g., MDT vs. Pharmacist)

### Outcomes (≥1 required)
- Falls (any fall, injurious fall, fall rate)
- Hospitalization (all-cause, unplanned, emergency admission)
- PIMs reduction (any validated criteria: Beers, STOPP/START, EU(7)-PIM, Japanese criteria, Taiwan criteria)

### Study Design
- Randomized controlled trials (parallel or crossover, ≥2 arms)
- Cluster-randomized trials
- Follow-up ≥3 months post-randomization

### Publication
- Full-text peer-reviewed publication
- Conference abstracts only if sufficient data for NMA (n, event rate, CI/SD)
- Any language (translate if needed)

---

## Exclusion Criteria
- Mean age <65 years
- Single-drug / single-class interventions (e.g., "stop statin trial")
- Non-pharmacist, non-MDT, non-CDSS education-only interventions
- No relevant outcome reported (falls/hospitalization/PIMs)
- Follow-up <3 months
- Observational, before-after, or quasi-experimental designs
- Duplicate publications (retain most complete/recent)

## Decision Rules for Borderline Studies
- Mixed-age populations: include if ≥75% aged ≥65 OR mean ≥65
- Mild cognitive impairment populations: include (medication management still relevant)
- Pharmacist as part of MDT: classify as MDT if ≥2 disciplines documented
- CDSS + pharmacist follow-up: classify as CDSS if the primary trigger is electronic
